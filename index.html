Hi! I am a senior majoring in Linguistics at The Ohio State University. My research interests include experimental semantics and pragmatics, as well as computational linguistics. I hope to use experimental and computational methods to investigate meaning in communication, especially implied meaning and pragmatic inference, and to explore how Large Language Models (LLMs) can extend our understanding of meaning and language more broadly.

My undergraduate thesis, Attempt Inference from Manage and Fail: Gradience and Context, is advised by Dr. Prerna Nadathur. Building on previous work showing that linguistic inferences are often gradient rather than binary, this project focuses on implicative verbs, especially manage and fail. I investigate how attempt inferences triggered by these verbs show gradient projective behavior across different contexts, including priors and QUD. For this project, we conducted a large-scale experiment with over 1,000 participants and developed a Rational Speech Act (RSA) model of the projective behavior of attempt inferences from manage and fail, aiming to offer a new perspective on the theory of implicative verbs.

My other projects include a study using word embeddings to investigate headedness distributions in Chinese compounds, accepted to the 38th North American Conference on Chinese Linguistics, and a small, RSA-based account of presupposition accommodation with again.

Outside of research, I love music, food, and photography. And if you happen to be interested in opera, we will probably have a lot to talk about :)
