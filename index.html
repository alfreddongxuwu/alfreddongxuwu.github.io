Hi! I am a senior majoring in Linguistics at The Ohio State University. My research interests include experimental semantics and pragmatics, as well as computational linguistics>.

My thesis, Attempt Inference from Manage and Fail: Gradience and Context, is advised by Dr. Prerna Nadathur. Building on previous work showing that linguistic inferences are often gradient rather than binary, this project focuses on implicative verbs, especially manage and fail. We investigate how attempt inferences triggered by these verbs show gradient projective behavior across different contexts, including priors and QUD.

My other projects include a study using word embeddings to investigate headedness distributions in Chinese compounds, to appear in the proceedings of the 38th North American Conference on Chinese Linguistics, and a small, RSA-based account of presupposition accommodation with again.
